# Ocean Warming and Acidification: Kelp Growth Analysis

## Project Overview
This project investigates the synergistic effects of climate change stressors—specifically ocean warming and ocean acidification—on the growth of *Macrocystis pyrifera* (Giant Kelp). Using a multivariate statistical approach, the research quantifies how increased temperature and elevated $pCO_2$ levels interact to impact kelp blade surface area and metabolic health.

## Key Research Questions
* **Synergistic Effects**: Do warming and acidification act as additive or synergistic stressors on kelp physiology?
* **Metabolic Costs**: To what extent does ocean acidification increase the metabolic cost of growth in temperate marine ecosystems?

## Technical Methodology
* **Experimental Design**: Implemented a factorial laboratory experiment simulating future climate scenarios (High Temp/High $pCO_2$).
* **Statistical Analysis**: Performed Multiple Linear Regression (MLR) and Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) to isolate variables.
* **Data Visualization**: Developed custom growth-curve visualizations and interaction plots using `ggplot2` to illustrate stressor correlations.

## Technical Stack
* **Language**: R
* **Libraries**: `tidyverse`, `ggplot2`, `stats`
* **Methods**: Hypothesis Testing (T-tests), Multiple Regression, Factorial Design

## Results
The analysis revealed a statistically significant reduction in blade surface area expansion under combined stressors compared to control groups. The findings suggest that while kelp exhibits some resilience to individual stressors, the interaction between temperature and acidification significantly impairs carbon fixation efficiency.
