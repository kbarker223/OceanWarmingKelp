# Ocean Warming and Acidification: Kelp Growth Analysis

## Project Overview
This project investigates the synergistic effects of climate change stressors—specifically ocean warming and ocean acidification—on the growth of *Macrocystis pyrifera* (Giant Kelp). Using a multivariate statistical approach, the research quantifies how increased temperature and elevated $pCO_2$ levels interact to impact kelp blade surface area and metabolic health.

## Key Research Questions
* [cite_start]**Synergistic Effects**: Do warming and acidification act as additive or synergistic stressors on kelp physiology? [cite: 5]
* [cite_start]**Metabolic Costs**: To what extent does ocean acidification increase the metabolic cost of growth in temperate marine ecosystems? [cite: 5]

## Technical Methodology
* [cite_start]**Experimental Design**: Implemented a factorial laboratory experiment simulating future climate scenarios (High Temp/High $pCO_2$). [cite: 5]
* [cite_start]**Statistical Analysis**: Performed Multiple Linear Regression (MLR) and Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) to isolate variables. [cite: 5]
* [cite_start]**Data Visualization**: Developed custom growth-curve visualizations and interaction plots using `ggplot2` to illustrate stressor correlations. [cite: 5]

## Technical Stack
* [cite_start]**Language**: R [cite: 5]
* [cite_start]**Libraries**: `tidyverse`, `ggplot2`, `stats` [cite: 5]
* [cite_start]**Methods**: Hypothesis Testing (T-tests), Multiple Regression, Factorial Design [cite: 5]

## Results
The analysis revealed a statistically significant reduction in blade surface area expansion under combined stressors compared to control groups. [cite_start]The findings suggest that while kelp exhibits some resilience to individual stressors, the interaction between temperature and acidification significantly impairs carbon fixation efficiency.
